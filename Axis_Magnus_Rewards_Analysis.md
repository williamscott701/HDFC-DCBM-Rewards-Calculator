## 5. Magnus Burgundy - Partner-Specific Value Guide

### 5.1 Understanding Mile/Point Valuations

**Important Concept:** The same miles from the same airline can be worth different amounts depending on **how you redeem them**. This is NOT about which partner you choose, but about how smartly you use those miles.

#### What do Conservative, Moderate & Premium mean?

| Valuation Type | What It Means | Example |
|----------------|---------------|---------|
| **Conservative** | Poor redemption - Economy class, bad availability, or paying more miles than needed | Booking a ₹8,000 domestic flight using 10,000 miles = ₹0.80/mile |
| **Moderate** | Decent redemption - Good economy class or basic business class deals | Booking a ₹15,000 flight using 10,000 miles = ₹1.50/mile |
| **Premium** | Excellent redemption - Business/First class where cash price is very high | Booking a ₹2,50,000 business class ticket using 1,00,000 miles = ₹2.50/mile |

**Think of it this way:** If you use 10,000 Singapore Airlines miles to book a ₹8,000 economy flight, you got ₹0.80 per mile (conservative). But if you use the same 10,000 miles to book a business class upgrade worth ₹25,000, you got ₹2.50 per mile (premium). **Same partner, different value based on how you use it!**

## 6. Travel Edge - Direct Booking with EDGE Points

Instead of transferring points to airline/hotel partners, you can use EDGE points directly on **Axis Travel Edge** portal to book flights and hotels. Here's how it compares.

### 6.1 What is Travel Edge?

Travel Edge is Axis Bank's travel booking portal where you can:
- Book flights (domestic & international) on any airline
- Book hotels worldwide
- Pay using EDGE Points, cash, or a combination (Points + Pay)

**Access:** [travel.axisbank.com](https://travel.axisbank.com) or via Axis Mobile App

### 6.5 When to Use Travel Edge

**✅ USE Travel Edge when:**
- You need to book **any airline** (not just partners)
- **Last-minute booking** with no award availability
- Booking **budget airlines** (IndiGo, SpiceJet, AirAsia) where partner transfer doesn't help
- You want **simplicity** over maximizing value
- Booking **domestic economy** flights where cash prices are low anyway
- You've already **maxed out transfer caps** for the year

**❌ AVOID Travel Edge when:**
- Booking **business/first class** (partner transfer gives 3-6x more value)
- You have time to plan and **find award availability**
- Booking with **premium partners** (Singapore, Qatar, Marriott)
- You want to **maximize your rewards value**

## 9. Important Notes & Considerations

### Transfer Fees
- ₹199 + GST per transfer transaction
- No minimum transfer amount for most partners
- Transfers are typically instant or within 24-48 hours

### Point Expiry
- EDGE REWARD Points do not expire as long as the card is active
- However, transferred partner miles/points follow partner program rules

### Caps and Limitations
- Caps are per Customer ID, not per card
- Multiple cards under same Customer ID share the cap
- Caps reset on January 1st each calendar year (from 2025 onwards)

### Devaluation Risk
- Partner programs can devalue miles/points anytime
- Airlines often increase redemption requirements
- Hotels may adjust category placements

### Best Practices
- Never transfer points speculatively
- Always check award availability before transferring
- Compare cash vs. points for redemptions
- Consider transfer bonuses and promotions
- Diversify across partners to manage risk
